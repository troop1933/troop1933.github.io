A Few More Details

"Good ideas are common;
what is uncommon are
people
who will work hard enough
to bring them about."
~ Ashleigh Brilliant

Troop 1933 does things a little different than most other troops.

We embrace technological change, rather than defame it. We are self-governing and boy-led. We emphasize the process rather than the goal. We are problem-solvers and think outside the box.

You came here for a reason.

Perhaps you are curious about what we are doing. Maybe you are looking for something. You might just be bored. The information below was written for the inquisitive, the seeker, the scout. Is that you?

May what you find help you along your way.

Troop Meetings and Outings

Every Boy Scout Troop has meetings and outings, and we are no different. Our meetings are primarily for skill instruction, advancement, and boy-led activities.

  • During the school year, we meet at the Presbyterian Church on Tuesday nights between 6 pm and 8 pm.
  • During the summer, we meet in Kalispell's parks on Saturday mornings.
  • The troop has one outing each month and one weeklong outing each year.

Trimesters: preventing burnout since 2013

Troop 1933 participates in a year-round program, but we take breaks too. Our calendar is divided into trimesters. We work hard at Scouting for three months, then take a one month break. April, August, and December are our inactive months.

Texting + Internet = Twitter

Every member can stay in touch with our weekly meeting schedule through the free web service, Twitter. (Click here to see our feed.) We show all our members how to get updates through texting or the Internet. No account is required and less email to boot!

Failure is Inevitable

No one likes to lose. In a society where acceptance is largely a matter of making people happy, there is little room for learning to deal with failure. Troop 1933 tackles it head-on, and more importantly, we discover how powerful failure can be in correcting how we think and do.
